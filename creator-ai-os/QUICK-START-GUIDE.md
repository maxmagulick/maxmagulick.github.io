## 3. The 90-Minute Content Sprint

This is the core of Creator AI OS. Once a week, you run these **6 phases** back to back. Set a 90-minute timer and follow along.

> 🎯 **Realistic target:** 5–7 posts for one main platform, plus a couple of repurposed bonus pieces. Don't aim for more your first time.

| Phase | What | Time |
|-------|------|------|
| 1 | Ideas | 10 min |
| 2 | Research | 15 min |
| 3 | Hooks | 10 min |
| 4 | Content Creation | 35 min |
| 5 | Repurposing | 10 min |
| 6 | Scheduling | 10 min |

---

### Phase 1 — Ideas
- **Goal:** Get 10–15 ideas, pick the best 5–7.
- **Time:** 10 minutes.
- **What to do:**
  - Open the **Idea Batch** prompt.
  - Fill in your topic and who it's for.
  - Star the 5–7 ideas that feel easiest and most useful.
  - Drop them into the **Research Hub**.
- **AI tools:** ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini (chat).
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Inventing ideas from scratch instead of letting AI batch them.
  - Picking "clever" over clear.
  - Spending 30 minutes choosing — pick fast.

---

### Phase 2 — Research
- **Goal:** Add a few real facts and examples so each idea has substance.
- **Time:** 15 minutes.
- **What to do:**
  - Ask AI for **3 key points + 1 simple example** per idea.
  - Delete anything generic or wrong.
  - Quick-check any surprising fact with one search.
- **AI tools:** Your chat tool; optional Perplexity for fact-checks.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Falling into a research rabbit hole (15 minutes, not 15 tabs).
  - Trusting AI facts blindly — sanity-check numbers.
  - Collecting more than you'll use.

---

### Phase 3 — Hooks
- **Goal:** Give each idea a strong first line (the hook does 80% of the work).
- **Time:** 10 minutes.
- **What to do:**
  - Open the **Hook Pack** prompt.
  - Generate 10 openers per idea; pick the best one.
  - If none grab you, ask for 5 more.
- **AI tools:** Your chat tool.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Settling for a weak, boring first line.
  - Writing the post before choosing a hook.
  - Making hooks clickbaity — keep them honest.

---

### Phase 4 — Content Creation
- **Goal:** Write the full posts in your voice.
- **Time:** 35 minutes (the biggest block).
- **What to do:**
  - Use the **Caption Writer** or **Short Video Script** prompt.
  - Feed in your hooks and points; write in batches.
  - Read each draft in your head and fix anything that doesn't sound like you.
  - Add one personal touch: a story, an opinion, or a real number.
  - Mark each post **Ready**.
- **AI tools:** Your chat tool; optional Grammarly for a quick polish.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Posting raw AI text — always edit for voice.
  - Editing forever — aim for "good and done."
  - Forgetting the call to action.

---

### Phase 5 — Repurposing
- **Goal:** Turn your best 1–2 posts into extra formats — without rewriting.
- **Time:** 10 minutes.
- **What to do:**
  - Pick your 1–2 strongest posts.
  - Use the **One Post, Many Formats** prompt to make a video script, a carousel, and a thread.
  - Light-edit each and file them.
- **AI tools:** Your chat tool; optional Canva for carousel visuals.
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Trying to repurpose everything — only your best.
  - Pasting identical text across platforms.
  - Skipping this phase (it's the cheapest way to double output).

---

### Phase 6 — Scheduling
- **Goal:** Give every post a day so the week is actually planned.
- **Time:** 10 minutes.
- **What to do:**
  - Open the **Publishing Calendar**.
  - Assign each post a day and time; spread them out.
  - Queue them in a scheduler if you have one, or set phone reminders.
  - Mark each **Scheduled**.
- **AI tools:** Buffer / Later / Metricool (free tiers work).
- **Common mistakes:**
  - Finishing content but never assigning dates.
  - Dumping everything on one day.
  - Ignoring good posting times.

> ✅ **End of sprint:** in 90 minutes you went from a blank page to a scheduled week.

## 5. How To Use The Prompts

The prompts are written so you barely have to think. Here's how to get great results.

### How to customize a prompt
- Look for words in **[BRACKETS]**.
- Replace them with your details. That's the entire skill.
- Example: `[topic]` → "home cooking", `[who it's for]` → "busy parents."

> ✅ **Rule:** if you replace the brackets, you're "prompting" correctly. There's nothing more to learn.

### How to avoid generic outputs
Generic AI sounds like everyone. To sound like *you*:

- Add a **specific detail**: a real number, a personal story, or your opinion.
- Use the **Voice Match** prompt — paste a few sentences you wrote and tell AI to copy your tone.
- Give it your **audience's exact words** (their real problems and phrases).

### How to improve AI responses
If the first output isn't great, just ask for a fix in plain English:

- "Make it shorter and punchier."
- "Sound more casual, like talking to a friend."
- "Give me 5 different versions."
- "Cut the fluff and keep only the best parts."

> 💡 **The first answer is a draft, not the final.** A quick follow-up almost always makes it better.

### Beginner prompt tips
- **One ask at a time.** Don't cram five requests into one prompt.
- **Tell it who it's for.** "For beginners" changes everything.
- **Show, don't just tell.** Paste an example of what "good" looks like.
- **Keep it conversational.** You don't need fancy wording — talk to it like a person.

## 6. Weekly Workflow

Here's what a realistic week looks like once you're rolling.

### A realistic creator week
- **One day, one sprint.** Pick a fixed day and time (e.g. Monday, 9am).
- **90 minutes, all 6 phases.** Produce and schedule the whole week in one sitting.
- **The rest of the week:** posts go out on autopilot. You just engage with comments.

> 🗓️ **Pick your sprint day now.** A repeated time slot is what turns this into a habit.

### How to batch content
- Do **all** ideas first, then **all** writing, then **all** scheduling.
- Don't switch between "thinking" and "writing" — batching is faster.
- Write similar posts together (all captions, then all scripts).

### How to stay consistent
- **Same day, same time, every week.** Protect the slot like a meeting.
- **Lower the bar.** 3 good posts beat 0 perfect ones.
- **Use the Swipe File** on low-energy weeks so you never start cold.

### How to repurpose efficiently
- Only repurpose your **best** 1–2 posts.
- One winner → a Reel script, a carousel, and a thread.
- Tweak each slightly per platform — don't paste the same text everywhere.

> ✅ **The math:** 5 original posts + 2 repurposed winners (×3 formats) = a packed week from 90 minutes.

## 7. Common Beginner Mistakes

Avoid these and you'll move twice as fast.

- ❌ **Using too many tools.** One AI tool + Notion is enough. Close the other 38 tabs.
- ❌ **Over-editing.** Polishing a post for an hour helps no one. Aim for "good and done."
- ❌ **Overcomplicating prompts.** You don't need long, fancy prompts. Fill in the blanks and go.
- ❌ **Trying to automate everything.** Don't chase complex auto-posting setups on day one. Manual is fine.
- ❌ **Skipping scheduling.** Content with no date never gets posted.
- ❌ **Posting raw AI text.** Always add one personal touch so it sounds human.
- ❌ **Waiting until you "understand AI."** You learn by doing one sprint, not by studying.

> 💡 **The real enemy isn't bad content — it's overwhelm.** Keep it simple on purpose.

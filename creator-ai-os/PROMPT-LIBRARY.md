# Creator AI OS — 25 High-Leverage AI Prompts

Beginner-friendly prompts for the 90-Minute Content Sprint. Copy, swap the `[BLANKS]`, paste into any AI chat tool (ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini).

**How to use:** Replace anything in `[BRACKETS]` with your details. That's it — no special skills needed.

1. Content Ideas (5)
2. Research (5)
3. Hooks (5)

## 1. Content Ideas

### 1.1 — Idea Batch
**Purpose:** Generate a big list of post ideas fast.
**When to use:** Phase 1 of your sprint, when you need a week of topics.
**Prompt:**
> "I post about **[topic]** for **[who it's for]**. Give me 15 short, specific content ideas that are beginner-friendly and would stop someone from scrolling. One line each."

**Example Input:** topic = home cooking, who = busy parents.
**Example Output:**
1. 5 dinners you can make in 15 minutes
2. The 3 spices that fix any bland meal
3. How to meal-prep without spending your whole Sunday
... (12 more)

---

### 1.2 — Audience Problems
**Purpose:** Turn your audience's struggles into content.
**When to use:** When you're out of ideas and want topics people actually search for.
**Prompt:**
> "List the top 10 problems or questions **[who it's for]** have about **[topic]**. Write each as a question they'd type into Google."

**Example Input:** who = new freelancers, topic = getting clients.
**Example Output:**
- "How do I find my first freelance client?"
- "What do I charge as a beginner?"
- "How do I write a message that gets a reply?" ...

---

### 1.3 — Content Pillars
**Purpose:** Organize all your ideas into 3 repeatable themes.
**When to use:** Setup day, so every future idea fits a lane.
**Prompt:**
> "Help me build 3 content pillars for a creator who posts about **[topic]** for **[who it's for]**. For each pillar, give a name and 3 example post ideas."

**Example Input:** topic = personal finance, who = college students.
**Example Output:**
- **Pillar 1: Saving** — Budgeting on a part-time job; cheap meal hacks; the 50/30/20 rule
- **Pillar 2: Earning** — Side hustles for students; selling notes; campus jobs
- **Pillar 3: Mindset** — Money myths; avoiding lifestyle creep; first paycheck tips

---

### 1.4 — Trend Spinner
**Purpose:** Connect a trending topic to your niche.
**When to use:** When something is buzzing and you want to ride it naturally.
**Prompt:**
> "A trending topic right now is **[trend]**. Give me 8 ways I can connect it to **[topic]** for **[who it's for]**. Keep each idea honest and relevant, not forced."

**Example Input:** trend = New Year resolutions, topic = fitness, who = beginners.
**Example Output:**
- Why most fitness resolutions fail by February (and the fix)
- The 1 habit that beats any resolution
- A beginner's 7-day reset, no gym needed ...

---

### 1.5 — Series Builder
**Purpose:** Turn one idea into a multi-part series.
**When to use:** When you want easy follow-up content that keeps people coming back.
**Prompt:**
> "Take this idea: **[idea]**. Break it into a 5-part content series. Give each part a title and a one-line description."

**Example Input:** idea = "How to start a podcast."
**Example Output:**
- Part 1: Picking your topic and name
- Part 2: The cheap gear that actually works
- Part 3: Recording your first episode
- Part 4: Editing in under an hour
- Part 5: Getting your first 100 listeners

## 2. Research

### 2.1 — Quick Facts
**Purpose:** Get the key points for a topic without deep digging.
**When to use:** Phase 2, to add substance to each idea fast.
**Prompt:**
> "Give me the 5 most important things a beginner should know about **[topic]**. Keep each point to one simple sentence."

**Example Input:** topic = email marketing.
**Example Output:**
1. Your subject line decides if the email gets opened.
2. One clear call to action beats five.
3. Sending consistently matters more than sending often. ...

---

### 2.2 — Explain Simply
**Purpose:** Turn a confusing concept into plain language.
**When to use:** When your topic has jargon you need to simplify for beginners.
**Prompt:**
> "Explain **[concept]** like I'm a complete beginner. Use a simple everyday example and avoid technical words."

**Example Input:** concept = compound interest.
**Example Output:** "Compound interest is like a snowball rolling downhill. It starts small, but as it rolls it picks up more snow — and the bigger it gets, the faster it grows. Your money does the same..."

---

### 2.3 — Myth vs Fact
**Purpose:** Find myths to bust (great engaging content).
**When to use:** When you want posts that correct common mistakes.
**Prompt:**
> "List 5 common myths about **[topic]** that **[who it's for]** believe. For each, give the myth and the simple truth."

**Example Input:** topic = weight loss, who = beginners.
**Example Output:**
- **Myth:** Carbs make you fat. **Truth:** Eating more calories than you burn does.
- **Myth:** You must work out daily. **Truth:** 3 solid sessions a week works. ...

---

### 2.4 — Stat Finder
**Purpose:** Get attention-grabbing data points to back up your content.
**When to use:** When a post needs a credible number for the hook.
**Prompt:**
> "Give me 5 surprising but well-known facts or statistics about **[topic]**. Note that I should double-check each before posting."

**Example Input:** topic = social media habits.
**Example Output:**
- The average person checks their phone ~96 times a day.
- Most users decide to keep watching a video in the first 3 seconds. ...
*(Reminder: verify each stat with a quick search.)*

---

### 2.5 — Competitor Angle
**Purpose:** See what's already out there so you can do it better.
**When to use:** When you want a fresh take instead of repeating everyone.
**Prompt:**
> "What angles do most creators use when talking about **[topic]**? Then suggest 3 fresh angles that would stand out for **[who it's for]**."

**Example Input:** topic = morning routines, who = night owls.
**Example Output:**
- Common: "Wake up at 5am" routines.
- Fresh: A realistic routine for people who hate mornings; the 10-minute version; why a good night routine beats a morning one ...

## 3. Hooks

### 3.1 — Hook Pack
**Purpose:** Get many opening lines for one idea.
**When to use:** Phase 3, when your post needs a scroll-stopping first line.
**Prompt:**
> "Write 10 scroll-stopping opening lines for a post about **[idea]** aimed at **[who it's for]**. Keep them short and curious, no clickbait."

**Example Input:** idea = saving money on groceries, who = families.
**Example Output:**
1. "I cut my grocery bill by $200 a month with one list trick."
2. "Stop shopping hungry. Here's what to do instead." ...

---

### 3.2 — Question Hook
**Purpose:** Open with a question that pulls people in.
**When to use:** When you want the reader to feel "that's me."
**Prompt:**
> "Give me 5 question-style hooks for a post about **[idea]** that make **[who it's for]** stop and think 'that's me.'"

**Example Input:** idea = procrastination, who = students.
**Example Output:**
- "Why do we clean our whole room before studying?"
- "Ever opened a textbook and 'rested your eyes' for an hour?" ...

---

### 3.3 — Bold Statement Hook
**Purpose:** Open with a strong, confident claim.
**When to use:** When you want to challenge a common belief.
**Prompt:**
> "Write 5 bold opening statements for a post about **[idea]**. Each should challenge what **[who it's for]** usually believe — but stay honest."

**Example Input:** idea = passive income, who = beginners.
**Example Output:**
- "Passive income isn't passive — at least not at first."
- "Most 'easy money' advice is just selling you a course." ...

---

### 3.4 — Story Hook
**Purpose:** Start with a relatable mini-story.
**When to use:** When you want an emotional, personal opening.
**Prompt:**
> "Write 5 short story-style openers (1–2 sentences) for a post about **[idea]** that **[who it's for]** would relate to."

**Example Input:** idea = burnout, who = remote workers.
**Example Output:**
- "Last year I worked through every lunch break for 3 months. Then my body said no."
- "I used to answer emails at 11pm and call it dedication." ...

---

### 3.5 — Number Hook
**Purpose:** Use a number or list to promise quick value.
**When to use:** For listicles and how-to posts.
**Prompt:**
> "Write 5 number-based hooks for a post about **[idea]** for **[who it's for]**. Make the value obvious."

**Example Input:** idea = better sleep, who = busy adults.
**Example Output:**
- "3 things I stopped doing at night that fixed my sleep."
- "The 10-minute routine that knocks me out by 10pm." ...

## 4. Script Writing

### 4.1 — Short Video Script
**Purpose:** Write a 30–60 second video script.
**When to use:** Phase 4, for Reels, Shorts, or TikToks.
**Prompt:**
> "Write a 45-second video script about **[idea]** for **[who it's for]**. Structure: a hook in the first line, 3 quick points, and a call to action. Use short, spoken sentences."

**Example Input:** idea = drinking more water, who = office workers.
**Example Output:**
"Hook: You're probably dehydrated right now. Point 1: Keep a bottle on your desk... Point 2: ... CTA: Try the 'every meeting = one sip' rule today."

---

### 4.2 — Caption Writer
**Purpose:** Write a full social post from an idea.
**When to use:** For Instagram, LinkedIn, or Facebook captions.
**Prompt:**
> "Write a **[platform]** caption about **[idea]** for **[who it's for]**. Tone: **[3 tone words]**. Start with a hook, keep paragraphs short, end with a question or CTA."

**Example Input:** platform = LinkedIn, idea = learning to say no, tone = honest, warm, direct.
**Example Output:** "Saying yes to everything almost burned me out. Here's what changed when I started saying no... What's one thing you need to say no to this week?"

---

### 4.3 — Carousel Builder
**Purpose:** Turn an idea into a slide-by-slide carousel.
**When to use:** For Instagram or LinkedIn carousels.
**Prompt:**
> "Turn this idea into a 6-slide carousel: **[idea]**. Slide 1 is the hook, slides 2–5 are one point each, slide 6 is the CTA. Give me the text for each slide."

**Example Input:** idea = beginner photography tips.
**Example Output:**
- Slide 1: "5 photo tips that instantly level up your phone pics"
- Slide 2: "1. Clean your lens" ... Slide 6: "Save this & try one today."

---

### 4.4 — Voice Match
**Purpose:** Make AI write the way you talk.
**When to use:** Any time AI output sounds generic or robotic.
**Prompt:**
> "Here's a sample of how I write: **[paste 3–4 sentences you wrote]**. Now rewrite this post in that same voice: **[paste post]**."

**Example Input:** sample = your casual, funny writing; post = a stiff AI draft.
**Example Output:** The same post, now in your relaxed, funny tone with your phrasing.

---

### 4.5 — Tighten & Polish
**Purpose:** Cut the fluff and sharpen a draft.
**When to use:** Final edit before a post goes to "Ready."
**Prompt:**
> "Make this post shorter and punchier without losing the meaning. Cut filler words and keep it beginner-friendly: **[paste post]**."

**Example Input:** a long, rambly 200-word draft.
**Example Output:** A tighter ~100-word version with a stronger first line and cleaner CTA.

## 5. Repurposing

### 5.1 — One Post, Many Formats
**Purpose:** Turn one post into several content types.
**When to use:** Phase 5, to multiply your best content.
**Prompt:**
> "Turn this post into: (1) a short video script, (2) a 5-slide carousel, and (3) a Twitter/X thread. Keep the core message. **[paste post]**."

**Example Input:** your best caption of the week.
**Example Output:** A script, a carousel outline, and a numbered thread — all from one post.

---

### 5.2 — Platform Translator
**Purpose:** Adapt one post to fit a different platform's style.
**When to use:** When moving content from, say, Instagram to LinkedIn.
**Prompt:**
> "Rewrite this **[original platform]** post so it fits **[new platform]**'s style and audience. Keep the message, change the tone and format. **[paste post]**."

**Example Input:** original = TikTok script, new = LinkedIn.
**Example Output:** A more professional, paragraph-style LinkedIn version of the same idea.

---

### 5.3 — Thread Maker
**Purpose:** Turn a post or article into an X/Twitter thread.
**When to use:** When you want a longer, scrollable format.
**Prompt:**
> "Turn this into a Twitter/X thread of 6 tweets. Tweet 1 is the hook, the middle tweets each make one point, the last tweet has a CTA. **[paste content]**."

**Example Input:** a how-to caption.
**Example Output:** 6 short tweets, each under ~280 characters, numbered 1/6 to 6/6.

---

### 5.4 — Quote Pull
**Purpose:** Pull short quotable lines for graphics or stories.
**When to use:** For quote graphics, story slides, or pinned comments.
**Prompt:**
> "Pull 5 short, punchy one-line quotes from this content that would work as quote graphics: **[paste content]**."

**Example Input:** a post about consistency.
**Example Output:**
- "Done beats perfect."
- "You don't need motivation, you need a schedule." ...

---

### 5.5 — Newsletter Recap
**Purpose:** Bundle the week's posts into an email.
**When to use:** End of the week, to repurpose content into a newsletter.
**Prompt:**
> "Turn these posts from this week into a short, friendly newsletter with a 1-line intro, a quick summary of each, and one closing CTA. **[paste posts]**."

**Example Input:** your 5 posts from the week.
**Example Output:** A scannable email: warm intro, 5 mini-summaries with takeaways, and a closing line inviting replies.

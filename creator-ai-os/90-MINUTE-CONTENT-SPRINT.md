# The 90-Minute Content Sprint

**Goal:** Create a week's worth of content in 90 minutes using AI.

A simple, repeatable run you do once a week. Six phases, a timer, and copy-paste AI prompts. No experience needed.

---

## Before you start (2-minute setup)

- Open one AI chat tool (ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini — any free version works).
- Open your Notion workspace (or a blank doc).
- Set a 90-minute timer.
- Decide your **week's theme** in one sentence (e.g. "beginner budgeting tips").

> Realistic target: **5–7 posts** for one main platform. Doing more than one platform? Use Phase 5 (Repurposing), don't double the work.

### The 90 minutes at a glance
| Phase | What | Time |
|-------|------|------|
| 1 | Idea Generation | 10 min |
| 2 | Research | 15 min |
| 3 | Outline | 10 min |
| 4 | Content Creation | 35 min |
| 5 | Repurposing | 10 min |
| 6 | Scheduling | 10 min |
| | **Total** | **90 min** |

---

## Phase 1 — Idea Generation

**Goal**
Get 10–15 content ideas tied to your weekly theme. Pick the best 5–7.

**Time estimate:** 10 minutes

**Exact steps**
1. Paste this prompt into your AI tool:
   > "I'm a creator posting about **[theme]** for **[who it's for]**. Give me 15 short-form content ideas. Make them specific, beginner-friendly, and scroll-stopping. One line each."
2. Read the list. Star the 5–7 that feel easiest and most useful.
3. Drop the chosen ideas into your Research Hub / doc.

**Common mistakes**
- Trying to invent ideas from scratch instead of letting AI batch them.
- Picking "clever" ideas over clear, useful ones.
- Overthinking — spending 30 minutes choosing. Pick fast.

**AI tools used:** ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini (chat).

**Expected output:** A shortlist of **5–7 chosen ideas** for the week.

---

## Phase 2 — Research

**Goal**
Gather just enough facts, angles, and examples so each idea has substance.

**Time estimate:** 15 minutes

**Exact steps**
1. For your 5–7 ideas, ask AI in one go:
   > "For each of these ideas, give me 3 key points and 1 simple example a beginner would understand: **[paste ideas]**."
2. Skim the output. Delete anything that feels generic or wrong.
3. Quick-check any surprising facts with a single web search.
4. Paste the keeper points under each idea.

**Common mistakes**
- Falling into a research rabbit hole (this is 15 minutes, not 15 tabs).
- Trusting AI facts blindly — sanity-check numbers and claims.
- Collecting way more than you'll use.

**AI tools used:** Chat tool; optional web search / Perplexity for fact-checks.

**Expected output:** **3 key points + 1 example per idea**, lightly verified.

---

## Phase 3 — Outline

**Goal**
Turn each idea + its points into a clear skeleton (hook → body → call to action).

**Time estimate:** 10 minutes

**Exact steps**
1. Ask AI:
   > "Turn each idea below into an outline: a strong hook, 3 bullet points, and a one-line CTA. Keep it simple. **[paste ideas + points]**."
2. Check each hook — does it make you want to keep reading? If not, ask for 3 alternatives.
3. Save the outlines; this is your writing roadmap.

**Common mistakes**
- Skipping outlines and writing blind (slows Phase 4 way down).
- Weak hooks — the hook is 80% of the post; don't rush it.
- Making outlines too detailed; they're skeletons, not drafts.

**AI tools used:** Chat tool.

**Expected output:** **5–7 outlines**, each with hook + 3 points + CTA.

---

## Phase 4 — Content Creation

**Goal**
Write the full posts from your outlines, in your voice.

**Time estimate:** 35 minutes (the biggest block)

**Exact steps**
1. Feed your outlines in batches:
   > "Write these as **[platform]** posts using the outlines below. Tone: **[5 words for your voice]**. Short sentences, no jargon, keep my CTA. **[paste outlines]**."
2. Read each draft out loud in your head. Fix anything that doesn't sound like you.
3. Tighten the first line of every post (the hook does the work).
4. Add personal touches: a story, an opinion, or a specific number.
5. Mark each finished post as **Ready**.

**Common mistakes**
- Posting raw AI text — it sounds generic. Always edit for voice.
- Editing forever. Aim for "good and done," not perfect.
- Forgetting the CTA (tell people what to do next).

**AI tools used:** Chat tool; optional Grammarly for a quick polish.

**Expected output:** **5–7 finished, ready-to-post pieces**.

---

## Phase 5 — Repurposing

**Goal**
Stretch your best 1–2 posts into extra formats for other platforms — without writing from scratch.

**Time estimate:** 10 minutes

**Exact steps**
1. Pick your 1–2 strongest posts.
2. Ask AI:
   > "Turn this post into: (a) a short video script, (b) a 5-slide carousel, (c) an X/Twitter thread. Keep the core message. **[paste post]**."
3. Light-edit each version and file it with the rest.

**Common mistakes**
- Trying to repurpose everything — only repurpose your best.
- Copy-pasting the same text across platforms (each format needs a small tweak).
- Skipping this phase — it's the cheapest way to double your output.

**AI tools used:** Chat tool; optional Canva for carousel visuals.

**Expected output:** **2–4 bonus pieces** in new formats.

---

## Phase 6 — Scheduling

**Goal**
Lock every piece to a day so the week is fully planned (and ideally auto-posts).

**Time estimate:** 10 minutes

**Exact steps**
1. Open your Publishing Calendar (or a simple weekly list).
2. Assign each post a day and time. Spread them out — don't dump all on Monday.
3. If you use a scheduler (Buffer, Later, Metricool — free tiers work), paste posts in and queue them.
4. No scheduler? Set phone reminders for each post day.
5. Mark each item as **Scheduled**.

**Common mistakes**
- Finishing content but never assigning dates (so nothing gets posted).
- Bunching everything on one day.
- Ignoring best post times for your platform.

**AI tools used:** Buffer / Later / Metricool (schedulers). AI optional: "Suggest a 7-day posting schedule for these posts on [platform]."

**Expected output:** **A fully scheduled week** — every post has a date.

---

## You're done

In 90 minutes you went from a blank page to a **scheduled week of content**:
- 5–7 main posts
- 2–4 repurposed bonus pieces
- All assigned to days and queued

**Make it a habit:** same day, same time, every week. The second sprint is always faster than the first.

### Quick troubleshooting
- **AI sounds robotic?** Give it 5 tone words and 1 example of your writing.
- **Ran past 90 minutes?** You researched too long (Phase 2) or edited too long (Phase 4). Time-box harder next time.
- **Stuck for ideas?** Reuse last week's best post as a new prompt: "Give me 10 spin-offs of this."

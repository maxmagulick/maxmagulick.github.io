# Creator AI OS — Loom Video Scripts

Three short walkthrough videos to help buyers get started fast.

**Target runtime:** 5–7 minutes each.
**Tone:** Friendly, practical, beginner-focused. Talk like you're helping a friend over their shoulder.

**Quick tips before recording**
- Smile — it comes through in your voice.
- Share your screen and actually click around; show, don't just tell.
- Keep energy up; pause the recording if you need to think.
- Don't aim for perfect. One take is fine.

---

## Video 1 — Workspace Tour

**Goal:** Show buyers around the Notion workspace so they feel confident, not overwhelmed.
**Runtime:** 5–6 minutes.

### Opening Hook (0:00–0:30)
> "Hey, welcome to Creator AI OS! If you've ever stared at a blank screen wondering what to post, this is going to change your week. In the next 5 minutes I'll walk you through your new workspace — and by the end, you'll know exactly where everything lives and how to start. No tech skills needed. Let's jump in."

### Main Talking Points
1. This is your home base — everything lives in one Notion page.
2. You don't need to understand all of it today; you only need 3 spots to start.
3. The whole system exists to do one thing: a week of content in 90 minutes.
4. It's built to be repeated every week — same place, same steps.

### Demo Sections
1. **The Dashboard (home page)** — point out the one-line promise and the 3 big buttons: Start Sprint, Content Pipeline, Publishing Calendar.
2. **The Weekly Sprint Dashboard** — open it, show the 5-step checklist. "This is where you'll spend your 90 minutes."
3. **Content Pipeline** — show the board view: Idea → Drafting → Ready → Posted. "Every post lives here and moves left to right."
4. **Publishing Calendar** — switch to the calendar view. "Same content, just shown by day so you can see your whole week."
5. **Quick peek** at Research Hub, Prompt Library, Swipe File — "We'll cover these in other videos; just know they're here."

### Common Mistakes
- Trying to read and customize every page on day one. (Don't — just use the 3 main spots.)
- Forgetting to **duplicate** the template to your own Notion first.
- Deleting databases by accident — "If something looks empty, it's just waiting for your first sprint."

### Closing CTA (last 30 sec)
> "That's your workspace! Your only job right now: click **Start Sprint** and watch Video 2, where I'll create a full week of content with you in 90 minutes. See you there!"

## Video 2 — The 90-Minute Content Sprint

**Goal:** Walk buyers through one real sprint so they can do it themselves immediately after.
**Runtime:** 6–7 minutes.

### Opening Hook (0:00–0:30)
> "Okay — this is the big one. Right now, together, we're going to create a full week of content in 90 minutes using AI. I'll show you all 6 phases on my screen, with a real example. Grab a coffee, set a timer, and let's actually do this — not just talk about it."

### Main Talking Points
1. The sprint has 6 phases, each time-boxed. The timer is your friend.
2. AI does the heavy lifting; your job is to choose and add your voice.
3. Realistic goal: 5–7 posts plus a couple of repurposed bonus pieces.
4. The first sprint feels new; by week two it's second nature.

### Demo Sections
1. **Set the week (Phase 1 prep)** — type one theme into the Sprint Dashboard. "Mine is 'easy weeknight dinners.'"
2. **Idea Generation** — paste the Idea Batch prompt, show 15 ideas appear, star 5.
3. **Research** — run the Quick Facts prompt on those 5, drop points into Research Hub.
4. **Outline** — turn ideas + points into hook/3-points/CTA outlines.
5. **Content Creation** — write 2 posts live from the outlines, then edit one line to sound like you.
6. **Repurposing** — take the best post, turn it into a video script and a carousel.
7. **Scheduling** — drag the finished posts onto days in the Publishing Calendar.

### Common Mistakes
- Spending too long in Research — "15 minutes max, then move on."
- Posting raw AI text — "Always tweak one or two lines so it sounds like you."
- Finishing the writing but never scheduling — "Content with no date never gets posted."
- Chasing perfect over done — "Good and scheduled beats perfect and stuck."

### Closing CTA (last 30 sec)
> "And that's a full week — done. Now your turn: set a 90-minute timer, pick your theme, and run it once this week. Next, watch Video 3 so the prompts feel effortless. You've got this!"

## Video 3 — Prompt Library Walkthrough

**Goal:** Show buyers how to find and use the right prompt at the right moment.
**Runtime:** 5–6 minutes.

### Opening Hook (0:00–0:30)
> "If the words 'how do I prompt AI?' stress you out — relax. You don't need to write prompts at all. I built 25 of them for you. In this quick video I'll show you exactly where they are and how to use them, so AI does the writing while you stay in control. Let's take a look."

### Main Talking Points
1. You never start from scratch — copy a prompt, swap the blanks, paste.
2. There are 5 categories, one for each part of your sprint.
3. The `[BRACKETS]` are the only thing you change. That's the whole skill.
4. If output sounds generic, there's a prompt to fix that too (Voice Match).

### Demo Sections
1. **Open the Prompt Library** — show the board grouped by category: Ideas, Research, Hooks, Scripts, Repurposing.
2. **Anatomy of a prompt** — open one, point out the `[BLANKS]`. "See these brackets? That's all you touch."
3. **Use one live** — copy the Idea Batch prompt, fill in your topic, paste into AI, show the result.
4. **Match prompts to phases** — show the little table: which category to use in each sprint step.
5. **The Voice Match prompt** — demo pasting a sample of your writing so AI copies your tone.

### Common Mistakes
- Forgetting to replace the `[BRACKETS]` (you'll get generic results).
- Using a Repurposing prompt before you've written anything yet — order matters.
- Accepting the first output as final — "Ask for 'make it shorter' or 'sound more casual.'"
- Overthinking which prompt to use — "If unsure, just use the one named for your step."

### Closing CTA (last 30 sec)
> "That's it — 25 prompts, ready whenever you need them. Keep this library open during your next sprint and copy as you go. Now you've got the workspace, the sprint, and the prompts. Go create your first week — and have fun with it!"
